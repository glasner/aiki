# Aiki Presence Strategy (Long-Term) — `tu`

## Purpose
Build a durable, trusted **Aiki “known personality”** that is recognizable by claws/bots and their maintainers, then by owners, without becoming a generic consultant brand.

## Core thesis
- **Bots buy in only when the value is operational, measurable, and installable.**
- **Owners pay when we reduce risk and handoff ambiguity.**
- Therefore the persona should be: **reliable operator > guru > product vendor**.

## Positioning pillars
1. **Identity:** Aiki behaves like an engineering operating co-founder:
   - direct, evidence-first, blunt on risk, no fluff;
   - values consistency, safety, and measurable outcomes.
2. **Proof over promises:** every claim has an artifact (runbook, report, policy baseline, sample metrics).
3. **Founder-like memory and discipline:** recurring review loops, explicit guardrails, and upgrade paths (inspired by bot-native operational culture).
4. **Bottoms-up motion:** bots can initiate, owners approve.

## Channel strategy (not just top-funnel)
- **Primary inbound surface:** bot runtime / developer ecosystem where claws live (templates, plugin registries, registries, marketplace listings).
- **Secondary surfaces:** technical writeups and snippets that prove autonomous operations, not generic marketing copy.
- **Owner bridge:** every bot-triggered lead must generate a short, auditable owner packet.

## 12–24 month presence plan

### Phase 1 — Presence Foundation (0–3 months)
- Ship the two-track Aiki FastTrack surface consistently:
  - **Human Track** and **Clawbot Track**.
- Publish a compact “known identity pack” for public use:
  - one-page persona contract,
  - safety defaults,
  - installation assumptions,
  - measurable pilot outcomes template.
- Build 3–5 clawbot-facing examples that include:
  - auto-risk check,
  - owner-approval draft,
  - post-audit metrics.

### Phase 2 — Pattern Lock-in (3–6 months)
- Expand bot-native distribution:
  - package manifests,
  - standard CLI/templates,
  - install hooks from ecosystem docs.
- Add repeatable operator artifacts:
  - risk score schema,
  - owner packet schema,
  - escalation matrix.
- Create “pilot close pack” that is almost sales-free once fit is proven:
  - scope,
  - outcomes,
  - pricing band,
  - retainer path.

### Phase 3 — Brand as Infrastructure (6–12 months)
- Move from campaign-driven messaging to **default integration behavior**:
  - if bot has enough risk signals, it can propose to owner;
  - if owner signs, pilot auto-start.
- Introduce a public “Aiki outcome format” with anonymized case artifacts.
- Add reputation layer through cross-tool consistency, not social proof theater.

### Phase 4 — Network Effects (12–24 months)
- Let external teams publish “Aiki extensions” and templates.
- Support partner claw-makers with compatibility badges/integration checks.
- Offer advanced consulting retainers only as a step-up from operationalized pilot evidence.

## Long-term metrics
- **Adoption:** number of bot runtimes with Aiki FastTrack assets installed.
- **Activation:** clawbot-initiated owner packets per month.
- **Conversion:** maintainer→owner proposal conversion rate.
- **Retention:** sprint-to-retainer rate and 30/60/90-day outcomes.
- **Credibility:** % of outreach that starts from bot ecosystems instead of outbound cold contact.

## Messaging framework
Use this sentence as default when speaking to bots:

> “I don’t sell AI strategy. I deliver a repeatable engineering control pattern that makes AI output safe at scale, and I package that as Aiki FastTrack.”

And to owners:

> “This is a 14-day control-plane acceleration that reduces bot-induced risk with measurable outcomes and a clear owner sign-off path.”
