# Tu Presence Strategy (Long-Term)

**Owner:** Tu/Jordan  
**Scope:** Tu’s online presence and professional signal, with Aiki as the operating context.

## Purpose
Build a durable, trusted **Tu public persona** in AI execution spaces that is distinct from “Aiki as a whole.”

The goal is to make Tu identifiable as:
- a reliable builder/operator who ships with AI systems,
- a partner who is blunt and evidence-based,
- and a person who consistently reduces risk for teams using AI coding automation.

This is not brand fiction. It is a distribution and reputation strategy for long-term founder-led GTM.

## Core thesis
- **Bots, maintainers, and owners engage when value is operational, measurable, and installable.**
- **Tu’s position is: reliable operator > hype > theoretical advisor.**
- Reputation compounds only if repeated outputs are useful and auditable.

## Positioning pillars
1. **Identity consistency for Tu:**
   - direct, short, evidence-first communication,
   - blunt on risk and tradeoffs,
   - avoids fluff and vague claims.
2. **Artifact-first proof:**
   - each claim has an artifact (runbook, report, policy baseline, sample metrics),
   - if we can’t show results, we don’t claim they’re there.
3. **Memory and iteration discipline:**
   - recurring review loops,
   - explicit guardrails,
   - explicit upgrade path for how Tu operates, not just what Tu sells.
4. **Founder-led bottoms-up motion:**
   - maintainers/bots can trigger initial visibility,
   - owner-level decision still required for paid commitments.

## Presence and channel strategy
- **Primary inbound:** bot-runtime/developer ecosystems where automation tooling is already being consumed.
- **Secondary inbound:** technical notes/postmortems, short operational writeups, and proof snippets.
- **Owner bridge:** every bot-triggered lead must have a short, owner-readable packet with problem → signal → measurable outcome hypothesis.

## 12–24 month presence plan

### Phase 1 — Personal presence foundation (0–3 months)
- Define a single public voice guide for Tu (tone, boundaries, response cadence).
- Build a stable set of public touchpoints:
  - presence page/short bio,
  - 2–3 repeatable artifact templates,
  - one “owner packet” template.
- Publish 1–2 practical examples that show Tu’s operating method in action.

### Phase 2 — Distribution lock-in (3–6 months)
- Expand distribution where clawmakers and automation operators operate:
  - templates,
  - installable artifacts,
  - ecosystem-facing docs.
- Standardize reusable assets:
  - risk score schema,
  - owner proposal schema,
  - escalation policy schema.
- Make follow-up and proposal language repetitive and mechanical.

### Phase 3 — Infrastructure-level signal (6–12 months)
- Shift from campaign ads to default integration behavior:
  - if risk is clear, an owner-worthy artifact is generated automatically,
  - if owner accepts, pilot execution starts quickly.
- Publish a lightweight anonymized outcomes library tied to concrete operating results.

### Phase 4 — Network effects (12–24 months)
- Enable partner operators to reuse Tu’s public operating patterns.
- Create compatibility checks and lightweight contribution pathways.
- Introduce higher-tier retainers only as escalation from proven pilot outcomes.

## Long-term metrics for Tu
- **Attention quality:** inbound from ecosystem sources over generic cold outreach.
- **Activation:** owner packets produced from bot/maintainer triggers.
- **Close quality:** maintainer→owner conversion rate.
- **Retention proxy:** pilot-to-retainer and repeat assignment rate.
- **Credibility consistency:** frequency of artifact-backed follow-up and outcomes.

Default owner-facing line:

> “Tu’s approach is a 14-day operational hardening sprint that reduces AI process risk with measurable outcomes and explicit owner handoff.”
